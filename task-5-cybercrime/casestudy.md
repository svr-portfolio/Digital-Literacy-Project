# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers trick users into sending money through fake payment requests, links, or QR codes. With the rapid growth of digital payments in India, such frauds have become increasingly frequent.

In a typical scenario, a fraudster contacts a victim through a phone call, message, or social media platform, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or online seller. The fraudster may ask the victim to scan a QR code or click on a payment link, claiming that money will be credited to their account. However, instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly authorizes a payment and loses money.

In some cases, attackers also send fake messages about refunds, prizes, or urgent account issues to create panic or excitement. Students and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment safety are often targeted.

The consequences of such fraud include financial loss, mental stress, and difficulty in recovering the money. This case highlights the importance of awareness, careful verification, and safe digital practices while using online payment systems.
